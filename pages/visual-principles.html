7. Colors, fonts, UI, UX

General rules of applying colors to a website:
  1. The color should match the content and the tone of the page. For example, you shouldn't apply pink to online shop with military footwear.
  2. Different colors have different moods. Red is love, energy, intensity. Yellow is about joy, intelect, attention, but it is good only for the things you don't look very often and for very long time, because then it can be tiresome. Green is connected with freshness, safety, growth. Blue means stability, confidence and calmness. And last but not least, violet is roylaty, wealth and femininity.
  3. We should use about 2 - 3 colors on our page.
  4. We can match colors basing on different rules, like triad or square. Another method of composing color palett are analogous colors. This is the way if we want harmonious design, which is good for matches of navbars and main content or for logos and their backgrounds. If we want strong contrast we should aim at complementary colors. They are not good for font - background mix, but great for logos and icons.
General rules of typography (choosing a font):
  1. Serif fonts are divided into 4 families: Old Style (e.g. Adobe Jenson), Transitional (e.g. Baskerville), Modern (e.g. Didot) and Slab-Serif (e.g. American Typewriter). This type of fonts we can use if we want emphesize that is something is serious, old-washioned (in a good way), respectible, traditional, stable, trustworthy, stylish etc. The more modern serif font, the bigger contrast between thin and thick parts of the letters.
  2. Sans-serif fonts are also divided into 4 families: Grotesque (e.g. News Gothic), Neo-Grotesque (e.g. Helvetica), Humanist (e.g. Gill Sans), and Geometric (e.g. Futura). They are characterized by numerous right angles and very high readability (especially Humanist fonts). Sans-serif fonts looks firendly, modern, contemporary, tender, straightforward.
  3. We should use two types of fonts on our websites, to make them more interesting, but at the same time not cluttered.
  4. You should never combine supermodern and superold fonts.
  5. Combining one serif and one sans-serif font is a good idea - for example one on the header and the other on main text.
  6. To make your website more interesting you can also use different font weights in headers, body text etc.
  7. The forbidden fonts: Comic Sans, Papyrus (unless it is for the Avatar movie), Viner and Curlz. Jokes aside, you can use them, but only for specific purposes when you know what you are doing and their tone matches the content.
User Interface Design rules:
  1. Hierarchy - the bigger, the more colorful will be seen first.
  2. Layout - should be diverse, with relatively small text blocks, each row of text should be from 45 to 75 characters long. Text should be also composed with images.
  3. Text alignment - every text block should be align in the same way, for example to the center or left, and not that one block is on the left, next in the center etc. We should also minimize the number of base lines, to whick we align text. Base lines could be for example grid mesh.
  4. White space - it should be add to website in a sensible matter, to expose content and make it feel more special, instead of cluterred one on another.
  5. We should adjust the UI to the target audience of our webpage.
User Experience Design rules
  1. Simpler is better.
  2. Follow the paths (habits) of users.
  3. Visual and functional coherence.
  4. Apply content in consonance with users' reading patterns.
  5. Design responsive websites, that will be beautiful on PC, laptops, tablets and mobile phones.
  6. Do not use the dark patterns: it is forbidden to trick the user into actions he or she would never voluntarily perform.
